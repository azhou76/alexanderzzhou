DECA

My efforts have allowed me to achieve 2nd place in the Travel and Tourism Team Decision Making event at the 2019 St. Louis District DECA competition and 3rd place in Buying and Merchandising Team Decision Making at the 2020 St. Louis District DECA competition. Ladue Mayor's Youth Council

I applied to be part of the Ladue Mayor's Youth Council in June of 2019 because I felt there existed a disconnect between the local youth and the city government. In addition, I felt a desire to not only represent the voices of high school students at Ladue Horton Watkins but also of today's youth in general. Since joining the Youth Council, I have worked directly with Ladue Mayor Nancy Spewak to tackle issues ranging from the "Lock it or Leave it" campaign aimed at raising local awareness about the outbreak of stolen cars in the area to the City of Ladue's next Comprehensive Plan to initiatives aimed at increasing community interaction through recycling efforts and community-wide parades.

I have helped directly in the planning of the City of Ladue's next Comprehensive Plan, giving input from a youth perspective as to what the overall vision and goals should be for the next decade and a half. However, our most important development has been creating the Ladue Student Opportunities Network. This network has directly connected local businesses to the community's youth through internship opportunities, stimulating small business while also providing crucial career experience for those interested. In addition to actively promoting the Ladue Student Opportunities Network through social media, I directly proposed and implemented the Standardized Test Tutoring program, creating a free ACT, SAT, and PSAT tutoring service for other local high school students during the COVID-19 lockdown.

Future Problem Solving and Scenario Writing

I began Future Problem Solving in late 2013, continuing until 2015. During that time, I collaborated with team members to analyze futuristic scenarios, identify potential challenges, and propose action plans to address the underlying issues.

Both teams I was apart of achieved great success, earning 1st place in the State Competition for our age group in 2014 and 2015 and going on to compete at the International Competition. Rapsodo

I officially began my internship with Rapsodo in the summer of 2020, after having previously done a project for them through my Sports and Entertainment Marketing class. Rapsodo is a baseball, softball, and golf technology company that aims at giving prospective athletes a better scope of where they stand against other amateurs as well as professionals. My specific position had me working with both the Marketing Team as well as the Golf Team, helping to record and analyze data from testing, reach out to potential retailers, and utilizing my Spanish abilities to translate blogs for the large Spanish-speaking demographic that follows baseball.

I have learned how to use and manage Customer Relationship Management software such as Salesforce as well as the process behind the development of products, from testing to outreach to marketing.
